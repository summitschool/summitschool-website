About Summit Church School

We exist to come alongside parents who have answered the call to educate their children at home — offering the structure, accountability, and Christ-centered community that makes the journey sustainable and deeply rewarding.

At Summit Church School, we believe homeschooling is more than education—it’s a lifestyle of learning, growth, and family connection.

Founded as a ministry of Summit Church in Rainbow City, Alabama, Summit Church School provides a legal church school cover for homeschool families throughout the state. We believe parents are the primary educators of their children and we are honored to walk with you in that sacred responsibility.

Whether you are just beginning or have been homeschooling for years, we offer the peace of mind that comes from professional recordkeeping, legal coverage, and a supportive community of like-minded families.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers about enrollment, records, and how we support your family.

Is homeschooling legal in Alabama?⌄
Yes! Homeschooling is legal in Alabama. Families may homeschool through a church school, private school, or with a private tutor who meets state requirements. Summit Church School operates in compliance with AL Code § 16-28-7 (2025).
What is a church school?⌄
A church school is a legal covering for homeschooling families. Parents teach their children at home while remaining enrolled in a church school that helps maintain records, provides support and help with accountability.
Do I need a teaching certificate to homeschool?⌄
No. Parents do not need a teaching certificate to homeschool through a church school in Alabama.
What subjects am I required to teach?⌄
Alabama law does not require or mandate what you teach. At SCS we are very accommodating for what your student needs. High school transcripts and college may require certain high school subjects. It is good practice for the core academic areas. Most homeschool families teach: Math, English/Language Arts, Science, Social Studies/History, Reading, and Electives such as art, music, foreign language, life skills, and more.
How many days do I have to homeschool?⌄
Alabama requires 180 instructional days each school year in public school. The state does not mandate this. However SCS advises you to do at least 140-160 days.
Do homeschool students receive grades?⌄
That is up to the parent. Many families keep grades and records, while others use alternative methods to track progress. How you choose to grade your students k-8th is up to you but in high school, letter grades and percentages are important for transcripts for college, military or workforce.
Can homeschool students earn a diploma?⌄
Yes. Students enrolled in SCS receive a physical diploma upon meeting graduation requirements. We also host complete graduation commencement ceremonies for students graduating from Pre-K, Kindergarten, and High School. Our high school diplomas are recognized as official and accepted by all 2-year and 4-year colleges and universities, as well as all branches of the military.
Can homeschool students go to college?⌄
Absolutely! Homeschool graduates regularly attend colleges, universities, trade schools, military programs, and workforce training programs.
Can homeschool students participate in sports and extracurricular activities?⌄
Yes! Homeschool students can participate in a variety of opportunities, including church leagues, community sports, co-ops, clubs, theater, scouting, art classes, and more.
Do I have to follow public school hours?⌄
No. One of the benefits of homeschooling is flexibility. Families can create a schedule that works best for their children.
How much does homeschooling cost?⌄
Costs vary widely depending on curriculum choices, activities, and resources. Many families homeschool on a modest budget, while others choose more extensive programs.
Do homeschool students have to take standardized tests?⌄
Alabama does not require annual standardized testing for homeschooled students and neither does Summit Church School, though some families choose testing to monitor progress.
What records should I keep?⌄
It’s a good idea to keep: Attendance records, Grades (if applicable), Samples of student work for yourself, Reading lists, and High school transcripts for older students.
Can my child return to public school later?⌄
Yes. Placement decisions are made by the receiving school, so keeping good records can help make transitions easier. SCS keeps records and works with the school.
Is homeschooling only for certain types of families?⌄
Not at all! Homeschooling families come from all backgrounds and choose to homeschool for many different reasons. Every family’s journey looks a little different.
What are the benefits of homeschooling?⌄
Many families appreciate: Flexible schedules, Individualized learning, Strong family relationships, Freedom to tailor education to a child’s needs and interests, and Opportunities for hands-on learning and real-world experiences.

At Summit Church School, we believe homeschooling is more than education—it’s a lifestyle of learning, growth, and family connection. Whether you’re just beginning your homeschool journey or have been homeschooling for years, we’re here to encourage, support, and walk alongside your family.
